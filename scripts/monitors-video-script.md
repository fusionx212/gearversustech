### ALIENWARE AW2725DF

**SUBTITLE:** ALIENWARE AW2725DF — THE GLOSSY TAX

**[SHOT: Alienware in dark room — looks incredible. Then same monitor in bright room — reflections visible.]**

**SUBTITLE (bullet points):**
- Best motion clarity on the market — 0.03ms
- 3-year burn-in warranty — Dell UK RMA is painless
- But: glossy coating is a mirror in bright rooms
- Text clarity worse than IPS — colour fringing on fine text
- No built-in speakers at £550+

**VO:** This is the best gaming monitor here. It's also unusable in the wrong room. The QD-OLED panel delivers true blacks and 360Hz at 1440p — that's the sweet spot. Dell's 3-year burn-in warranty is best in class, and their UK RMA process is genuinely painless. But the glossy coating reflects everything. If you have a window behind you, you'll see yourself more than the game.

**[SHOT: Text document on Alienware screen. Macro shot showing colour fringing.]**

**SUBTITLE:** QD-OLED subpixel layout. Notice the colour fringing on text.

**VO:** Text clarity is worse than equivalent IPS. The subpixel layout causes colour fringing on fine text. If you work on this monitor as well as game, you'll notice it. Buy this if you game in a light-controlled room and don't use it for productivity. Otherwise, keep watching.

### AOC Q27G3XMN

**SUBTITLE:** AOC Q27G3XMN — THE VALUE STORY OF 2026

**[SHOT: AOC monitor. Dark scene showing Mini-LED dimming zones. Then blooming demonstration.]**

**SUBTITLE (bullet points):**
- Mini-LED with 336 dimming zones at £270–300
- Near-OLED contrast with zero burn-in risk

- But: VA viewing angles, blooming around bright objects

**SUBTITLE:** VA viewing angles. Colours shift when you move your head.

**VO:** The VA panel has narrower viewing angles — colours shift when you lean to the side. Mini-LED blooming is visible around bright objects on dark backgrounds — your mouse cursor on a black screen has a subtle halo. And AOC's OSD uses physical buttons instead of a joystick. But at £270 to £300, these are the right compromises. This panel would have cost £500 two years ago.

### LG 27GP850-B

**SUBTITLE:** LG 27GP850-B — THE SAFE BET FROM 2021

**[SHOT: LG monitor. Generic desk setup. Clean but unremarkable.]**

**SUBTITLE (bullet points):**
- Nano IPS — 98% DCI-P3, factory calibrated
- 165Hz native, 180Hz overclocked
- £220–280 — cheapest here, and it shows
- But: IPS glow, terrible contrast, HDR is decorative
- Five-year-old technology in a fast-moving field

**VO:** This panel launched in 2021. It's still good. Nano IPS with excellent colour accuracy out of the box — 98% DCI-P3. 165Hz native, overclockable to 180Hz. LG's build quality and warranty support are consistently reliable. It's a safe, boring, reliable purchase.

**[SHOT: Dark game scene on LG. IPS glow visible in corners.]**

**SUBTITLE:** IPS glow. Blacks look grey. Contrast ratio below 1000:1.

**VO:** But the contrast is poor — blacks look grey in dark scenes. The HDR400 badge is marketing, not a feature — there's no local dimming, so HDR does essentially nothing. And 180Hz overclock disables variable overdrive, so you'll see inverse ghosting. At £220 to £280, it's a known-good quantity. But you're buying five-year-old panel technology in a field that's moved significantly.
