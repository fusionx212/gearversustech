**[SHOT: HyperX headset. Overlay: "120 HOURS" pulsing. Then someone wearing them for hours — comfortable.]**

**SUBTITLE (bullet points):**
- 120-hour battery — not a typo, not an exaggeration
- Memory foam + leatherette — same formula since Cloud II, it works
- 53mm drivers — warm, consumer-friendly sound
- But: no Bluetooth — 2.4GHz only
- No simultaneous audio — gaming OR phone, not both

**VO:** 120 hours of battery life. You'll charge this thing twice a month with daily gaming. The memory foam ear cushions are the same proven formula HyperX has used since the Cloud II — you can wear this for six-plus hours without heat or pressure points. The 53mm drivers are tuned warm and consumer-friendly — explosions have weight, footsteps are audible without being piercing.

**[SHOT: USB dongle plugged into PC. Then phone with "no connection" graphic.]**

**SUBTITLE:** 2.4GHz only. No Bluetooth. No phone connection.

**VO:** But there's no Bluetooth. You can't connect this to your phone, Switch, or Steam Deck without the USB dongle. The Razer BlackShark V2 Hyperspeed at the same price does 2.4GHz and Bluetooth simultaneously — take a call while gaming. This can't. And the warm sound signature means competitive FPS players who want clinical, flat audio for precise footstep positioning will prefer the SteelSeries.

### STEELSERIES ARCTIS NOVA 5

**SUBTITLE:** STEELSERIES ARCTIS NOVA 5 — THE WIRED MIC KING

**[SHOT: SteelSeries headset. Close-up of the ClearCast microphone. Someone talking — clean audio waveform on screen.]**

**SUBTITLE (bullet points):**
- Best microphone under £100 — your Discord friends will notice
- Neutral sound signature — ideal for competitive FPS footstep audio
- SteelSeries Sonar EQ — free, system-wide, game-specific presets
- But: wired only — no wireless variant under £100
- Shallow ear cups — if your ears stick out, they press against drivers
- Non-detachable 3.5mm cable — if it snags and breaks, headset is dead

**VO:** This is the headset for people who care about how they sound on Discord. The ClearCast 2.X microphone is genuinely the best mic in this roundup — your friends will notice the difference immediately. The neodymium drivers are tuned for a neutral, accurate sound signature — you hear games as the audio engineers intended, not with a bass shelf. Competitive FPS players, this is your pick.

**[SHOT: Someone with larger ears trying them on. Discomfort visible. Then cable close-up — non-detachable.]**

**SUBTITLE:** Shallow ear cups. Non-detachable cable. Two dealbreakers.

**VO:** But the ear cups are shallow. If your ears stick out more than average, they'll press against the driver housing — try before you buy. The 3.5mm cable is non-detachable on the headset side — if it snags and breaks, the headset is dead unless you're handy with a soldering iron. And Sonar software is Windows-only — Mac and console users get no EQ.

### CORSAIR HS80 RGB WIRELESS

**SUBTITLE:** CORSAIR HS80 — THE AUDIOPHILE'S BUDGET PICK

**[SHOT: Corsair headset. Close-up of the broadcast-grade mic. Dolby Atmos logo on screen.]**

**SUBTITLE (bullet points):**
- Best wireless microphone under £100 — broadcast-grade
- Dolby Atmos for Headphones included — not a trial, not an upsell
- 50mm neodymium drivers — wide soundstage for closed-back
- Floating headband — distributes 370g well
- But: 50hr battery with RGB off, 20hr with RGB on
- iCUE software required — memory leak history on some builds
- Heaviest here at 370g — noticeable after 3–4 hours

**VO:** Corsair genuinely over-specced the microphone on this headset — it's one of the best wireless mics at any price under £150. Dolby Atmos for Headphones is included — not a trial, not an upsell, full Atmos license. The 50mm drivers are tuned for a surprisingly wide soundstage for a closed-back design — directional audio is better than it has any right to be.

**[SHOT: iCUE software. RGB on headset — looks cool but pointless since you can't see it.]**

**SUBTITLE:** RGB on a headset you can't see. 50hr battery → 20hr with lights on.

**VO:** But the battery life drops from 50 hours to around 20 with RGB on. The RGB is pointless on a headset you can't see while wearing — turn it off. iCUE is required for EQ and Dolby Atmos setup, and iCUE has a documented history of memory leaks on some Windows builds. At 370 grams, this is the heaviest headset here — you'll feel it after three to four hours.

### LOGITECH G435 LIGHTSPEED

**SUBTITLE:** LOGITECH G435 — THE BUDGET TRAP

**[SHOT: Logitech G435. Someone puts them on. Initially smiles at the weight. 90 minutes later — rubbing ears in pain.]**

**SUBTITLE (bullet points):**
- £45–60 for dual wireless (2.4GHz + Bluetooth) — cheapest here
- 165g — half the weight of the Corsair. Genuinely forget you're wearing them
- Made from recycled plastic — only brand with published environmental data
- But: on-ear design, not over-ear — cups sit ON your ears
- After 90 minutes your ears WILL ache. This is the dealbreaker
- 18-hour battery — worst here. Charging every 2–3 days
- No 3.5mm jack — when battery dies, headset is a paperweight

**VO:** £45 to £60 for dual wireless — 2.4GHz and Bluetooth — is genuinely good value. 165 grams — you will forget you're wearing these for the first hour. And Logitech is the only brand here with published environmental data if that matters to you.

**[SHOT: Close-up of on-ear cups on adult ears. Pressure visible. Red circle highlighting the problem.]**

**SUBTITLE:** On-ear. Not over-ear. Adult ears do not fit comfortably.

**VO:** But these are on-ear, not over-ear. The cups sit ON your ears, not around them. After 90 minutes, your ears will ache. This is the dealbreaker for most adults. The 18-hour battery is the worst here — charging every two to three days. Fabric ear pads transmit sound both ways — people next to you will hear your game audio. And there's no 3.5mm jack — when the battery dies, the headset is a paperweight until charged. If your ears are small enough for on-ear — and you're on a tight budget — it works. For everyone else, save up.
